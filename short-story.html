Memory Lane

A standalone short piece

It's so quiet in here, but you're still laughing at my jokes in my head.
You're still hanging on my lips.
You're still the one who hugs me and slips a paper ring onto my finger.

It’s strange — the way I can fly back into our memories while gravity keeps me pinned to this bed, tears streaming down my face for all we could’ve been.

It’s not like I haven’t moved on at all.
But for the past three years, you’ve lived rent-free in the corners of my mind.
Us — our young, silly love.
Your kisses.
Your warm blood beating through your arms while I rested my face there.

Has it really been that long?

I’ve spent years practicing how I’d act if we ran into each other again.
Would I scream at you?
Would I smile and say we made the right choice, that we found people better suited for us?
Or maybe — just maybe — you’d tell me you were wrong.

Most days, memories flood me so heavily that I drown before I realize I’m walking the old streets — the park where you held my hand for the first time and my ears went hot, the terrace where we ate ice cream and laughed like we were immortal.

Then there are moments when a scent hits me — that same perfume you loved — and I swear, I chased a stranger for two blocks just to figure out its name.

I loved you.
God, I did.
And a part of me always will.

I remember how you used to treasure the letters I wrote you.
I’ve written hundreds more since you left…
but you never came back to claim them.

Even in a room full of people, solitude finds me.
It sits beside me.
It crowns me.

And even though it hurts, I keep walking down memory lane — because that’s the only place I can still touch you.
Still kiss you.
Still crumble in your arms.
The only place where you still love me back.

It’s nice, isn’t it?
Loving someone completely.
Promising the world.
Believing in a future together.
